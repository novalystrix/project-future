# Historical Crisis Patterns — Quick Reference

*Extracted from the main analysis for easy comparison*

## Pattern Matrix

| Crisis | Duration | Trigger | Peak Job Loss | Recovery Time | What Held Value |
|--------|----------|---------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Great Depression (1929) | 10 years | Speculation → bank failures → policy error | 25% unemployment | 25 years (stocks) | Farmland, gold, cash, bonds |
| Dot-Com (2000) | 2.5 years | Speculative tech valuations | Tech-specific | 5 years (broad), 15+ (NASDAQ) | Profitable tech, real estate, bonds |
| 2008 Financial Crisis | 2 years acute | Housing bubble → bank leverage | 10% unemployment | 5 years (stocks), 8-12 (real estate) | Treasuries, gold, cash |
| Japan Lost Decades (1990) | 30+ years | Real estate/stock bubble | Slow grind, not acute | 34 years (stocks never recovered until 2024) | Cash, bonds, foreign assets |
| Industrial Revolution | 60-80 years | Mechanization of manual labor | Entire professions destroyed | 2-3 generations | Land, new skills, urban real estate |
| Weimar Hyperinflation (1921) | 2 years | War debt + money printing | Universal impact | 2 years (currency reset) | Real estate, gold, foreign currency |

## Common Patterns

1. **Government always responds** — but often too late, wrong, or creates new problems
2. **Cash is king during deflation** — but deadly during inflation
3. **Real assets survive** — farmland, gold, productive property
4. **Recovery happens** — but timelines range from 2 to 80 years
5. **The prepared buy at the bottom** — liquidity during panic is the ultimate advantage
6. **New technology survives** — the speculation around it doesn't

## Key Differentiator: AI vs. Past Disruptions

Past disruptions replaced *physical* or *routine* labor. AI replaces *cognitive* labor — the work humans retreated to after every previous disruption. There's no obvious "next rung" on the ladder this time.
