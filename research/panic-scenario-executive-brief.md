## 🌾 Real Assets (15%) — The Farm & Your Apartments

### The Farm 🏡

This is both a lifestyle goal and the best panic-scenario asset. In every historical collapse, farmland held value because people need to eat.

| Factor | Guidance |
|--------|----------|
| Location | Central/Northern Israel — Galilee, Golan, Sharon region. Far enough from city to be real farmland, close enough to be practical. |
| What to look for | Water access (well or reliable irrigation), arable soil, 10-50 dunams minimum. Olive groves, citrus, or mixed-use. A house or buildable plot. |
| Budget | Moshav land with house: ₪2-5M depending on area. Agricultural land only: ₪100-300K per dunam in good areas. |
| Timing | **Buy within 12-18 months** — before the crisis. Farmland prices only go up in panic scenarios. |

> In the 1930s US, farmland dropped initially but recovered fastest and kept families alive. In Weimar Germany, landowners survived while the middle class was wiped out. A farm is your ultimate insurance — it produces food, it's physical, and it can't be printed away.

### Your City Apartments

| Action | Reasoning |
|--------|-----------|
| ✅ **KEEP** — Don't sell | Israeli residential real estate is structurally supported by limited land supply, immigration, and housing shortage. Even in a panic, the floor is high. |
| Lock in fixed-rate mortgages | If any apartments have variable-rate loans (prime-linked), refinance to fixed NOW. In inflation, fixed debt is a gift. In deflation, variable debt kills you. |
| Ensure positive cash flow | Rents may dip 10-20% in a recession. Make sure rental income covers mortgage + expenses even at reduced rents. |

> ⚠️ Israeli real estate is illiquid in a crisis. Don't count on selling apartments quickly if you need cash. That's what the Cash Reserve bucket is for.
